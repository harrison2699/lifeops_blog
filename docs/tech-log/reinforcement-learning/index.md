# Reinforcement Learning

**Reinforcement learning (RL)** here means learning from **trial and error** with a (possibly sparse) reward signal: value methods, policy gradients, actor–critic, model-based RL, and sim-to-real transfer when rewards and dynamics are approximate.

## Scope for this section

- Sample efficiency, **exploration**, and off-policy stability  
- Simulators, domain randomization, and **reward engineering vs reward learning**  
- Safety constraints (CBF-style shields, constrained RL) when RL meets hardware
