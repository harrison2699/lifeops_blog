# 2026-W18 Tech Log

Week of **2026-05-04** — integration-heavy week on a manipulation stack (ROS 2 Humble, custom hardware interface).

## Summary

Brought a **velocity-saturated torque controller** online in simulation, then chased real-robot discrepancies caused by timing jitter and an underestimated friction model. Ended the week with repeatable pick trials on three representative objects.

## What I Worked On

- Wrapped an existing inverse dynamics block so it could run at a fixed **500 Hz** control loop with explicit deadline monitoring.  
- Instrumented the hardware bridge: message age histograms for `sensor_msgs/JointState` and command topics.  
- Tuned a simple **Coulomb + viscous** friction estimator from logged free-motion coast-down data.  
- Paired with perception to align grasp frame conventions between offline planning and online servoing.

## Problems I Solved

- **Executor starvation**: a diagnostic node accidentally lived in the same callback group as the controller; splitting groups restored predictable scheduling.  
- **Sign convention drift** between URDF inertia frames and the vendor driver: added a one-time self-test that commands micro-motions and checks predicted vs measured acceleration direction.  
- Pick failures on a glossy box traced to **latency** in the depth pipeline, not the grasp planner; shifted to a conservative pre-grasp hover with shorter visual servo distance.

## What I Learned

- Histograms of message age beat averages for spotting “mostly fine, sometimes awful” DDS behavior.  
- When friction is wrong, **integral windup** in outer loops looks like “mysterious instability at contact,” not a classic tuning problem.

## Technical Notes

- Outer loop: Cartesian impedance with a wrench cap; inner loop: PD on joint torque with saturation aware of motor thermal limits (conservative caps during bring-up).  
- Logged fields included commanded torque, measured torque (where available), tracking error, and executor overrun flags for post hoc alignment with video.

## Public Insight

Reliable manipulation is less about a clever grasp metric and more about **time determinism** from camera to actuator. If your perception stack is honest about latency, the rest of the stack can be honest too.
