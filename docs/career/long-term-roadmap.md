# Long-Term Engineering Roadmap

This note is a compass, not a contract. It exists to keep decisions **consistent over years** as roles, teams, and platforms change.

## Becoming a stronger robotics engineer

Depth beats breadth when depth includes **the fundamentals**: kinematics and dynamics, linear systems intuition, probability, and the courage to read papers implementation-first. I want each year to add at least one **durable skill**—for example, better contact modeling, sharper ROS 2 performance instincts, or cleaner evaluation discipline.

## Building technical assets

**Assets** are reusable: open-source tools, high-quality notes, talks, and code templates that make the next project faster. The portfolio pages here are a deliberate archive so lessons do not evaporate when a repository goes read-only.

## Writing consistently

Weekly **Tech Logs** keep honesty high and overhead low. Monthly, I promote one or two threads into **Articles** when they clear a simple bar: would I be comfortable revisiting this explanation in public a year later? Writing is part of engineering quality, not a side hobby.

## Preparing for a long engineering career

Long careers reward **compound learning**: small improvements that stack. That means protecting focus time, learning to say no gracefully, and choosing roles where the feedback loop includes real hardware or realistic proxies—not only slides.

## Balancing depth, health, family, and financial stability

- **Depth** requires uninterrupted blocks; I treat them as calendar first-class citizens.  
- **Health** is load-bearing: sleep, strength, and eyesight directly affect debugging quality.  
- **Family** is non-negotiable presence time; late-night heroics are a planning failure, not a badge.  
- **Financial stability** reduces fear-driven decisions; boring savings and insurance questions deserve annual attention like a dependency upgrade.

## Review cadence

Re-read this roadmap quarterly. Adjust one paragraph at a time rather than rewriting from scratch—small edits track real life better than dramatic manifestos.
